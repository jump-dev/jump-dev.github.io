We are pleased to announce the winners of the 2026 JuMP-dev prize.
Continuing our tradition started in [2025](/prize/jump-dev-2025), this year's
prize is a jump rope. The prize committee consisted of Oscar Dowson, Joaquim
Garcia, Benoît Legat, and Miles Lubin.

This year there are two winners:

 * Daniel Nguyen, for his engaging talk "DisjunctiveProgramming.jl’s New Suite
   of Solution Methods."

 * Sophie Lequeu, for her talk "Adding array support for JuMP’s Automatic
   Differentiation" and for her work on ArrayDiff.jl, which is an experimental
   library that adds vector-valued automatic differentiation (AD) to JuMP.
   Support for vector-valued nonlinear functions is one of the oldest and
   most-frequently requested features in JuMP, and we look forward to a future
   pull request that makes this an official feature!

Congratulations, and happy JuMPing!
